# Social Media Marketing

Promoting brands, products, and ideas through social platforms — **engagement, not one-way broadcast**. The fifth pillar of the Fortisyn marketing knowledge base, alongside [[40-Resources/marketing/copywriting|Copywriting]], [[40-Resources/marketing/email-marketing|Email Marketing]], [[40-Resources/marketing/seo-fundamentals|SEO Fundamentals]], and [[40-Resources/marketing/growth-hacking|Growth Hacking]]. This is the discipline the [[90-System/agents/auron-agent|Auron Agent]] operationalizes for every Fortisyn brand.

## Core Idea

Social media reaches stakeholders — customers, fans, press — through two-way conversation. Companies win by being present, consistent, and authentic in the right places, not by shouting on all of them.

## Content Strategy

Two modes, kept in balance:

- **Planned content** — structured, approval-gated, scheduled. Safer but slower (~72h cycle). The Fortisyn default.
- **Unplanned content** — spontaneous, reactive to trends. Faster, riskier. Use sparingly and on-brand.

Vary formats so the feed never goes stale; repurpose one idea across platforms with platform-native phrasing.

## Engagement

- **Passive** — monitor mentions, feedback, sentiment as market intelligence.
- **Active** — reply, converse, partner with influencers and creators.

Engagement is the part **automation should not fully replace** — see [[20-Skills/marketing/social-media-automation|Social Media Automation]].

## Paid vs Organic

Organic reach has declined sharply (Facebook business reach fell from ~16% in 2012 to ~2% by 2014). Treat organic as relationship-building and social proof; treat paid as targeted amplification of what already works organically — the [[40-Resources/marketing/growth-hacking|growth-hacking]] "scale what works" loop.

## Metrics & KPIs

Engagement rate (likes/comments/shares), reach/impressions, follower growth, click-through to site, conversions, sentiment, and review ratings. Track, then feed learnings back into timing and voice.

## How It Connects to the Other Pillars

- **[[40-Resources/marketing/email-marketing|Email Marketing]]** — social drives traffic to email signups; email is the owned channel social rents attention for.
- **[[40-Resources/marketing/seo-fundamentals|SEO]]** — social signals correlate strongly with organic search performance; shares amplify content reach.
- **[[40-Resources/marketing/copywriting|Copywriting]]** — every post is concise, platform-specific copy; tone and honesty protect brand credibility.
- **[[40-Resources/marketing/growth-hacking|Growth Hacking]]** — UGC, referral loops, and data-driven virality all run on social.

## Ethics

Honesty about intentions, no false advertising, respect for user privacy and data. Brand credibility is the long game.

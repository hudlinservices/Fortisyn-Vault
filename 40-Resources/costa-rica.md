## Relocation — Digital Nomad Visa

Costa Rica's Digital Nomad / Remote Work Visa is the most relevant entry path for a foreign-income earner like Roy:

- **Income threshold:** ~US$3,000/month for an individual, ~US$4,000/month for a family, from foreign sources.
- **Duration:** 1 year, renewable (some guidance markets a 2-year framing via renewal).
- **Tax:** Under Costa Rica's **territorial tax system**, foreign-sourced income is **not taxed locally** — visa holders are explicitly exempt from Costa Rican income tax on foreign earnings, plus import-tax exemptions on work equipment brought in.
- **Constraints:** Cannot work for Costa Rican companies. Must carry long-term residency health insurance (travel insurance is not accepted).
- **Timeline:** ~15 days initial review; up to 90 days to enter and finalize after approval.

The territorial system is the key strategic fact: a holding structure earning income from foreign clients (e.g. AES Engineering via [[10-Projects/innovatience/_corporate-hub|Innovatience]]) is favorably positioned.

## Business & Corporate Setup

- **100% foreign ownership** permitted for both entity types; no director/shareholder residency requirement.
- **Entity choice:** **S.R.L.** (Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada — uses quotas, streamlined, popular for SMBs) vs **S.A.** (Sociedad Anónima — shares, better for governance/transfer, favored by investors/developers).
- **Compliance:** Registered office + resident agent required. Since 2020, all entities file the **RTBF** (Registro de Transparencia y Beneficiarios Finales), disclosing any beneficial owner holding ≥15% to the Central Bank.
- **Timeline/cost:** Registry filing 5–7 business days; full incorporation incl. tax registration and banking 3–6 weeks; first-year cost ~US$2,000–5,000.
- **Banking caveat:** Banks often require an in-person (or video) interview with a director, and **frequently decline pure holding companies with no local operations** — relevant to how [[10-Projects/fortisyn/_corporate-hub|Fortisyn]]/[[10-Projects/auroralegacy/_corporate-hub|Aurora Legacy]] would bank locally. See [[40-Resources/holding-company-trust-structure|Holding Company & Trust Structure]] for the umbrella's trust→holding→operating design.

## Tech & Connectivity Infrastructure

Costa Rica is now viable for serious remote tech work:

- **Fiber** is standard in nomad hubs and major cities (50–200 Mbps), ~US$30–60/month. 4G coverage is excellent; 5G expanding.
- **Variability:** beach towns have less reliable connectivity than San José / Escazú — a real consideration for La Dolce Niente's location and for hosting/uptime-sensitive work.
- This connectivity gap is exactly why the [[40-Resources/sff-llm-build|SFF LLM Machine]] (local compute, no cloud dependency) and Hudlin Services' failover stack matter for a Costa Rica base.
- **Hubs:** Tamarindo (coworking/networking), San José (best infrastructure, international airport, banking).

## Cost of Living (2026)

- Comfortable single-person budget: ~US$2,000–3,000/month.
- Housing: ~US$600 (smaller towns) to US$1,200+ (San José / coastal hotspots). Beach towns run 20–30% higher than inland.
- Costa Rica ranked **#3 globally** in digital-nomad search interest in 2026 — driven by the visa, fiber, 0% foreign-income tax, and lifestyle.

# MindTechArt

**MindTechArt** is Roy Hudlin's personal-brand framework: the deliberate fusion of **Mind** (mindfulness), **Tech** (technology), and **Art** (creativity). It is the connective philosophy under [[10-Projects/royhudlin/_corporate-hub|the Roy Hudlin personal brand]] and the throughline that explains why a low-voltage-systems consultant also publishes meditation books, shoots photographic poetry, and builds AI agents. It appears across 11+ vault pages but had no page of its own — this is that page.

## The Three Pillars

| Pillar | What it means for Roy | Vault evidence |
|--------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Mind** | Mindfulness, stillness, slowing down a fast-paced life; helping people with always-on lives find peace | *The Art of Stillness* (Mindful Way Trilogy), [[10-Projects/mercovaretail/junglemeditation\|Jungle Meditation]] |
| **Tech** | Technology as a tool for growth, not distraction; AI agents, Django apps, the [[40-Resources/sff-llm-build\|SFF LLM Machine]] | [[10-Projects/pythonslayers/slf-for-roy\|SLF for ROY]], [[90-System/agents/auron-agent\|Auron Agent]] |

The companion concept is **Narrative Alchemy** — storytelling as a timeless art that binds the three pillars into a single voice.

## The Core Tension It Resolves

MindTechArt is interesting precisely because its pillars *seem* opposed: technology is the thing most often blamed for the burnout and distraction that mindfulness tries to cure. Roy's framing — **"technology as a tool for growth, not distraction"** — is the resolution, and it maps directly onto an established design movement.

## Grounding: Calm Technology

The MindTechArt thesis is the personal-brand expression of **calm technology**, a design philosophy formalized by **Amber Case** (building on 1995 Xerox PARC research, *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design*, 2015):

- **Definition:** technology "that informs but doesn't demand our focus or attention" — interaction happens in the user's *periphery* rather than at the center of attention.
- **Core principles:** technology shouldn't require all of our attention; it should move in and out of attention only when needed; it should *empower the periphery* (like noticing a car engine only when something's wrong).
- **Goal:** tech as a way to *supplement* human life rather than dominate it — reducing reliance on the digital.
- Case founded the **Calm Tech Institute** (2024) and a **Calm Tech Certified™** program scoring products on attention, periphery, durability, light, sound, and materials.

This is the design-theory backbone for the otherwise-fuzzy "Tech" pillar: MindTechArt products (meditation apps, the SLF companion, AI agents with human-approval gates) should be *calm* — they inform and assist without colonizing attention.

> Adjacent movements that reinforce the same idea: **digital minimalism / digital wellbeing**, **slow living**, and the "attention economy" critique. MindTechArt is Roy's branded, creativity-forward take on this lineage.

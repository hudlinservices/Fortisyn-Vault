## The Three Practices (and how they differ)

| Practice | What it is | What the evidence shows | Time horizon |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mindfulness meditation** | Sustained, non-judgmental attention to the present moment | Reduces stress, anxiety, depression; lowers cortisol; improves emotion processing and cognitive performance | Long-term sense of peace |
| **Breathwork** | Structured breathing (e.g. exhale-focused cyclic sighing) | Exhale-focused breathing produces *greater, faster* mood improvement and lower respiratory rate than meditation alone | Immediate relief |
| **Energy work** | Coaching practice Roy bundles with the above on Jungle Meditation | (Practitioner framing, not clinical) | — |

The practical takeaway Roy's content can lean on: **breathwork is the fast on-ramp** (immediate arousal reduction), **meditation is the long game** (durable equanimity). Jungle Meditation already pairs both — "guided meditation, breathing techniques, and energy practices."

## Flow: the bridge to the "Tech" pillar

Roy's writing repeatedly connects mindfulness to **flow** — Mihály Csíkszentmihályi's state of energized, fully-absorbed focus (see the export conversations *Flow and Mindfulness*, *Mindfulness and creativity flow*). The evidence backs the link: present-moment awareness and breathwork both *increase access to flow* by minimizing distraction. This is the hinge that makes [[40-Resources/mindtechart|MindTechArt]] coherent — mindfulness is not opposed to productivity/creativity; **it is the precondition for the deep focus that technical and creative work require.** It also dovetails with [[40-Resources/mindtechart|calm technology]]: tech that stays in the periphery is tech that protects flow.
